    Key Findings

    Evidence-based insights from 14 years of CDC BRFSS data across all U.S. states and territories.

    National & Geographic Trends
    📈
    Steady upward trend over 13 years

    National adult obesity rose from 27.6% in 2011 to 34.0% in 2024 — a 23% relative increase. No year showed a decline.

    🗺️
    Southern states carry the highest burden

    West Virginia (41.2%), Mississippi (40.1%), Arkansas (40.0%), Louisiana (39.9%), and Alabama (39.2%) all exceed 39% in 2023.

    🏙️
    Urban and Western states perform better

    DC (23.5%), Colorado (24.9%), and Hawaii (26.1%) consistently rank lowest — metro-heavy and high-activity states show lower rates.

    Socioeconomic Disparities
    💰
    Income is inversely protective

    Adults earning <$15k have 38.1% obesity vs 34.7% at $75k+. Lower income is consistently associated with higher risk.

    🎓
    Education is strongly inversely linked

    College graduates have a 28.7% rate vs 36.4% for those without a high school diploma — a 7.7 percentage point gap.

    Racial & Behavioral Patterns
    ⚠️
    Large racial disparities persist

    Hawaiian/Pacific Islander (44.6%), Non-Hispanic Black (40.2%), and American Indian/Alaska Native (39.8%) face the highest rates. Asian adults have notably lower rates (13.8%).

    🏋️
    Inactivity strongly correlates with obesity

    States with highest leisure-time inactivity (Puerto Rico, MS, AR, WV) are also among those with the highest obesity prevalence.
