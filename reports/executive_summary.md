# Executive Summary

## Objective

Identify where adult obesity burden is highest, which population differences are most decision-relevant, and how public-health teams can measure the impact of targeted action.

## What the analysis shows

- Adult obesity prevalence increased over the observed period.
- Geography, education, income, and race/ethnicity show material descriptive variation.
- The male–female difference is small relative to several other group differences.
- Location and population stratification provide useful signals for prioritization models.

## Recommended action

Pilot targeted activity and nutrition programs in high-burden locations. Use a 10% enrollment improvement, 15% activity-guideline improvement, 20% tailored-message lift, and 10% equity-gap reduction as initial evaluation targets. These are operational targets—not effects proven by the observational analysis.

## Limitations

Survey estimates may be affected by self-reporting, sampling, and aggregation. Confidence intervals and local context should accompany decisions. The models are not suitable for individual clinical use.
